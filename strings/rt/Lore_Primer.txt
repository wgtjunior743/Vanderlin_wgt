<p><strong><em><span style="font-size:115%"> Welcome to Vanderlin! </span></em></strong></p>
<p> This lore primer serves as an introduction to Vanderlin's lore and world. <br>
While it's not required reading, leadership roles may find this information useful to roleplay their positions. This, or anyone else interested in playing a character with more knowledge about the setting. Not all information is available here, and some secrets still lie within item descriptions, inscriptions, or booklets- it's a primer, not a compendium. </p>
<hr>
<strong><span style="font-size:105%"> Some quick vocabulary/concepts important to the setting: </span></strong>
<p><strong> Psydonia </strong> - The world Vanderlin takes place in, named after its creation god, Psydon. It is currently the year 2515 after His death. </p>
<p><strong> Faience </strong> - The continent Vanderlin takes place in. Shaped by His hand. </p>
<p><strong> Lux </strong> - The core link of divinity a person holds; the soul. A sliver of Psydon present within each species touched at one point by divine hand. It is said that it carries the power of a single, focused miracle of life- yet its removal dooms a soul never to pass into the underworld. </p>

	<summary><strong><span style="font-size:130%"> AREAS OF FAIENCE </span></strong></summary>



	<p><strong>  Isle of Enigma </strong><em> - "Once the trade center of the world, now a festering cancer of spilled oil, shrapnel, and bomb-shattered soil."  </em>
	<br></p>

	<details>
		<summary><em> Points of Interest </em></summary>

		<p><strong> - Town of Rockhill </strong><br>  A once-bustling trade center colloquially known as Roguetown. Now a warzone, the last-standing settlement against the armada of automatons. </p>
		<p><strong> - Kingdom of Heartfelt </strong><br>  The prosperous head of technological progress within the Isle of Enigma, experiencing disaster as their automaton constructions turn against them and ravage their lands.  </p>
	</details>
	<br>


	<p><strong>  Peninsula of Vanderlin </strong><em> - "The new trade capital of the world."  </em></p>

	<details>
		<summary><em> Points of Interest </em></summary>

		<p><strong> - City of Vanderlin </strong><br> A canyon settlement on a nearby peninsula to the East of the Isle of Enigma. Reaping the boons of both, Vanderlin is a city of trade and prosperity. Built over the first-discovered opening to the Tomb of Matthios, it attracts adventurers from all over Psydonia to delve within and seek fortune.  </p>
		<p><strong> - County of Daftmarsh </strong><br> A thrice-sunken castle, now a town-on-stilts built from its ruins. Daftmarsh guards the river delta directly North of Vanderlin. It often serves as a safe cove for pirates, providing Vanderlin merchant ships safe passage in exchange for the Crown's averted eyes. </p>
		<p><strong> - Mire of the Terrorbog </strong><br> A fertile and marshy area of the peninsula, overgrown and suffering an invasion of beespiders.  </p>
		<p><strong> - Shire of Murderwood </strong><br> Ancestral Forest bearing many glades, overrun by villains, knaves, and other lawlessness. A testament to Dendor's madness, its constant-shifting thicket stretches across Western Vanderlin. </p>
		<p><strong> - Malum's Anvil </strong><br> A jagged mountain filled with ores, treasures, and lava. Likewise a cacophony of horrid goblins, beasts, and other misfortunes. The crater of Astrata's fallen shard still scars its peak. </p>
	</details>
	<br>


	<p><strong>  Kingsfield  </strong><em> - "The pride of humenity. Crown of Psydonia"	</em></p>

	<details>
		<summary><em> Points of Interest </em></summary>

		<p><strong> - City of Kingsfield </strong><br>  Massive city built upon temperate forests far, far North to the Isle of Enigma. Kingsfield is the most prosperous and populated city of the world, not to mention the major cultural export. It is not to be confused with the state, Kingsfield, which encompasses it. Kingsfield rail systems span all across Western Faience, of no small part due to Heartfeltian artificers' effort. </p>
	</details>
	<br>


	<p><strong>  Tundras of Wintermare </strong><em> - "The largest humen-accepted elven territory, though most land is covered in tough permafrost."  </em></p>

	<details>
		<summary><em>Points of Interest</em></summary>

		<p><strong> - City of Rosewood </strong><br> The capital of the Wintermare Empire, located near its Southeastern inland sea. Houses world-reknown Noccian academies, it is widely considered the magic capital of the world. </p>
		<p><strong> - White Palace Pass </strong><br> One of few passageways through The Howling Mountains between Grenzelhoft and Rosewood. A place of great tension and prior conflict. </p>
		<p><strong> - Noc's Crown </strong><br> A towering peak hiding ruins of past civilization.  </p>
		<p><strong> - Morvael Sink </strong><br> A frigid marsh. Old temples to Zizo lie in ruin.  </p>
		<p><strong> - Snowdust Glade </strong><br> An enchanted forest bearing the dangers of unchecked fae and abandoned summons.  </p>
	</details>
	<br>

	<p><strong>  Necral Forests </strong><em> - "Ancestral forests scattered around Psydonia. The ancient homes of the elves." </em></p>

	<details>
		<summary><em> Points of Interest </em></summary>

		<p><strong> - Amber Hollow </strong><br> At the center of the Northwestern Redwoods, a ring of gnarled, interlaced roots sits atop a great earthen mound. Within which lies the largest Western entrance to Subterra. Escaped hollowkin experiments, and later harpies, call this settlement their home. Almost all hollowkin can trace their ancestry back to this maw. While hostile to outsiders, this serves as a major point of defense against dark elven forces. </p>
		<p><strong> - The Tree of Psydonia </strong><br> A great sequoia dwarfing all others, located far Northwest of Amber Hollow. It is said to be a memorial created by Psydon Himself to honour Necra's original death. Elves revere this landmark, despite its unsettling, skull-like bark.  </p>
	</details>
	<br>

	<p><strong>  The Barrows </strong><em> - "Former boreal forests called home by the snow elves, now a deadland of ruin and decay following the ascension of Zizo. Home to the great war front against her undead armies." </em></p>

	<details>
		<summary><em> Points of Interest </em></summary>
		<p><strong> - The Realm of Z </strong><br> The northern parts of The Barrows are colloquially known as 'The Realm of Z' due to her nigh-complete control. Few, if any, survivors remain- though rumours circulate that a last bastion settlement in the Northern, mountainous frostlands presses on despite all odds.  </p>
	</details>
	<br>

	<p><strong>  Subterra </strong><em> - "A vast cave system underground with its own unique biosphere of hostile flora and fauna. It once housed an ancient empire of Ravoxian dark elves, but now serves as the conquered core of Zizo's evil matriarchy." </em></p>

	<details>
		<summary><em> Points of Interest </em></summary>

		<p><strong> - Zizo's Claw Marquisate </strong><br> Located below Amber Hollow, marking the farthest West reach of Subterran caverns. Serves as a forward operations base for dark elven forces and slaves as they prepare yet another attempt at breaching the surface.  </p>
		</details>

<details>
	<summary><strong><span style="font-size:130%"> FACTIONS OF FAIENCE </span></strong></summary>
	<br>
	<strong><span style="font-size:115%"> MAJOR POWERS </span></strong>
	<br><br>
	<details>
		<summary><strong> KINGSFIELD EXPANSE </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<em>The Crown of Psydonia</em>
		<br><br>
		Kingsfield spans along the Northwest of Faience, housing the cultural center of the world in its massive cities and impressive population. Unlike many of the other humen-majority populations, its inhabitants and history are defined by kindness rather than conquest. A certain demographic of Kingsfieldians are identifiable by their odd pronunciation of certain Imperial words and inclusion of 'eh?' after a comment or question. Species from all over the world would visit and establish themselves within these cities, including a non-insignificant population of dark elves before Zizo's conquest. They are, perhaps, the most tolerant of half-drow as a result- and as such are subject to mockery by the rest of Faience.
		<br><br>
		Kingsfield's prominence was built from tactical allyship and infrastructure development through industrialization. Through close cooperation with Heartfelt, Kingsfield railways span across the Western side of the entire continent, stretching from the tip of Kingsfield's tundras to the bottom of Vanderlin's peninsula. Kingsfield's population, notable in arts and culture worldwide, is of course largely Eoran and Xylixian in patronage; however, scholars of Noc account for a close third.
		<br><br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> EMPIRE OF WINTERMARE </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<em>Noc's Embraced, The Elven Empire, The Arcyne Tundra</em>
		<br><br>
		Wintermare is the largest elven power in Psydonia, often the only territory marked on humen maps. Unfortunately, most of its territory is covered in unusable Northern permafrost despite its size. Nevertheless Wintermare remains incredibly influential, with most of its Southern cities producing renown magicians and Noccian scholars. Kingsfieldian rail links along the Southern mountain ranges, providing vital resources to an otherwise tense border zone between the elven empire and Grenzelhoft below.
		<br><br>
		Abandoned settlements and ghost towns along the border of Wintermare stand as graves and memorials to wars and genocides of the past. What populated locations remain are riddled with discarded and potentially active weapons of war. What don't... after Zizo's rise, no one goes there anymore.
		<br><br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> GRENZELHOFT IMPIRATE </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<em>The Black Empire, The Empty Throne, Empire of the Trident</em>
		<br><br>
		Grenzelhoft is an imperialist state carved across the Northeast of Faience through political upheaval and military action. They are known mainly for their striking cuisine, honourable, loyal mercenaries, and their overwhelming monopoly over black powder. Despite a largely humen-supremacist culture, dwarves are embraced and regarded as the friends and builders of Psydon's vision.
		<br><br>
		Grenzelhoftians proudly assimilate all humens who come under their heel into their Empire, integrating local and cultural identities into their fold before crushing them in favour of their own. Grenzelhoft bears the largest armies in the world and incredible military might, which come under vast scrutiny and disgust as they become increasingly desperate to upkeep their power. Their history is defined by crusades, elven genocide, and general aggression in the supposed name of Psydon and His return to the throne. Orthodox Psydonism is the dominant religion. Critics and pagans are persecuted.
		<br><br>
		Their people cannot remember a time when the Empire was not at war, and many have served within its military forces to escape the intense poverty of its lower-class. A world-scale conflict between Grenzelhoft and its neighbours was narrowly avoided in the early 2100s by the rise of a new common enemy: Zizo. Grenzelhoft's forces, explosives, and firearms now supply the Eastern front with vital support under the explicit command of Grenzelhoft's cabal of sun-averse counts and countesses.
		<br><br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> ZALADIN DYNASTY </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<em>The Red Sands, Empire of the Dunes, Merchants of the Pasha</em>
		<br><br>
		A grand-spanning, mercantilist empire which found prosperity and riches from spice export. Zaladin is home to some of the worlds greatest wonders, though the labourers who built them were directed by force and threats rather than passion. The dominant ideologies are Astratanism and Xylixianism. Xylixian freemen and Astratan order-keepers clash often in mortal representations of divine conflict. Zaladians have grown numb to the possibility of a civil war, and these skirmishes have simply become another aspect of daily life.
		<br><br>
		Zaladin was known as 'Zybantine' whilst under Grenzelhoftian occupation. It was a title forced upon them rather than chosen, and was violently rejected alongside the repellance of Grenzelhoft's Southern crusades by Zalad armies. In modern times, the term has become considered a grave and bigoted insult.
		<br><br>
		Within desperation after the failed conquest, Zaladin's dwindling forces turned to conscription of prisoners-of-war, the first of their slave-armies, forcefully enlisting many captured Grenzelhoftians. These troops are sent to support the front lines of allied Valorian states to the North against Zizo's armies in the Barrows. Regardless of origin, Zaladin's signature "Desert Riders" are a force to reckon- especially when mounted atop their twain-humped pegasi.
		<br><br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> VALORIAN RUPUBLIAS </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<em> The Freestates, Vineyards of Valoria, The Lords of Coin </em>
		<br><br>
		Valoria is a land of constant strife, bickering city-states with a number of conflicting interests. Yet they bear some pride of their lands, banding together to repel the invading Grenzelhoft Empire out of shared desire for freedom. With conflict comes innovation; the Valorian lands developed pride in their revolution of blade combat strategy and stance. Largely followers of Ravox, they value honour and duels as an expression of status.
		<br><br>
		Valoria is renown for their alcohol exports and trade, seconding only Zaladin as most-sought goods.
		<br><br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> DWARVEN CONFEDERACY </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<em> The Great Throngs, The Braided Confederacy, Seven Hammers </em>
		<br><br>
		Located in the mountains of continents all over Psydonia. The political aspects stem from federations bearing unique dwarven clans, leaderships, industries, and cultures. The largest establishment is within the great mountain range to the Southeast of Zaladin's deserts. Dwarves and humen empires have established a long history of allyship. Whilst dwarves often choose neutrality in humen-on-humen conflict, a dwarven face is a trusted face to most humens.
		<br><br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> ELVEN ENCLAVES </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<em> Keepers of Psydonia, Retreats of the Ancient Age, Forgotten Guides of Death </em>
		<br><br>
		The declining population has led Elves to retreat to compact and isolated communities outside of Wintermare. They defend their remaining homes in natural wonders around Psydonia to the death and without mercy. Subdivided into distinct cultures such as Wood, Dark, Sand, Mountain, Sea, and countless more. Whilst once there lived a culture of snow elves, Zizo's ascension resulted in an all-but complete genocide of the tribe. Most elven settlements are not even recognized, much less their their territories represented within modern humen maps.
		<br><br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> ORC & GOBLIN THRONES  </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<em> Crusaders of Graggar, Graggarspawn, Heirs of Psydonia  </em>
		<br><br>
		Spontaneous fortresses and towns of Crimsonland origin, established via nomadic means. These encampments take advantage of natural resources and nearby settlements to raid, conquer, consume, and then move on to more promising targets. They are ever-moving hordes of gluttony and rage which constantly follow a pattern of great loss followed by a steady regrowth. Most of these forces are still recovering from the most recent Goblin War.
		<br><br>
		A misogynistic and self-destructive culture to its extreme, scholars report that several terms of language have been developed just to describe masculinity. Any sane individual loses focus after an orc rambles about &Sigma;-class masculinity or whatever newest scam Graggarspawn have begun pushing. Records are sparse as a result.
		<br><br>
	</details>
	<br><br>
</details>


<details>
	<summary><strong><span style="font-size:130%"> MYTHOS OF PSYDONIA </span></strong></summary>
	<br>
	<strong><span style="font-size:115%"> THE OLD GOD </span></strong>
	<br>
	<em> The only good god is dead. He holds our rightful worship. </em>
	<br><br>
	<details>
		<summary><strong> Orthodox Psydonism </strong></summary>
		<br>
		The overwhelming majority of Grenzelhoft's church of Psydon. Orthodox Psydonites believe that Psydon lives still, though divided into nine pieces. They are intent to tear down The Ten so that the true God may reform, however long it takes. They hold a deep hatred towards Necra for her butchery and betrayal of Psydon.
		<br><br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> Progressive Psydonism </strong></summary>
		<br>
		A schism within the orthodox church of Psydon, this progressive faction believes that worship is possible through The Ten. Rather than striving to cut down The Ten, progressive Psydonites instead look for signs of His will expressed through the pieces left behind.
		<br><br>
	</details>

	<hr>
	<strong><span style="font-size:115%"> THE DIVINE PANTHEON </span></strong>
	<br>
	<em> The time of Psydon has passed, The Ten are righteous. Necra leads us to salvation. </em>
	<br><br>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> Astrata </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<b><span style="display:block; color:#e5d569; font-size:130%"> "THY DEVOTION IS NO MERE SUGGESTION. I DEMAND IT." </span></b>
		<br>
		<em>The Sun Queen, The Tyrant of The Ten.</em>
		<br><br>
		Goddess of light and fire. Her domain encompasses the sun, healing, and most importantly the revival of the dead. She was crafted from the head of Psydon.
		<br><br>
		Astrata leads The Ten with an iron fist.  She demands obedience and respect as she leads both the mortal and divine worlds through calamity after calamity. As time passes, her grip only gets tighter and her patience for heresy thinner. She simply cannot spare her focus. Since the 2430s, she fills the cycles of darkness with periodic flares to light the nite.
		<br><br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> Noc </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<i><span style="display:block; color:#82a1d9; font-size:120%">"Dreams are thy gateway to wisdom, if thou art worthy." </span></i>
		<br>
		<em>The Moon Prince, The Keeper of Magick.</em>
		<br>
		God of knowledge and magick. His domain encompasses the moon, study, record-keeping, and self-improvement. He was crafted from the silver helm of Psydon.
		<br><br>
		Noc guides scholars, often nobles, through life dedication in order to master the ways of arcyne magick. Since the ascension of Zizo, he has only grown more strict with who are worthy of practicing this gift. He is known to wear the most fabulous of dresses.
		<br><br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> Abyssor </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<span style="display:block; color:#8b2d39; font-size:120%"> "Don't be fishin' deeper than ye can catch." </span>
		<br>
		God of the sea and storms. His domain encompasses all within the oceans, water, and blood. He defends the natural world alongside his brother, Dendor. Abyssor was crafted from the drained blood of Psydon.
		<br><br>
		With the destruction of the natural world, both brothers of nature were driven mad. While Dendor was struck with insanity, Abyssor encompasses the rage of the sea. Woe be to any who offend him.
		<br><br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> Dendor </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<span style="display:block; color:#74875a; font-size:120%"> "Prophecy? Eh... what prophecy? Madness? Drivel? Nonsense, the lot of it!" </span>
		<br>
		God of nature and beasts.  His domain encompasses the wilds on the surface, and all flora and fauna within. He does not claim any Subterran life due to its hellish corruption. Dendor was crafted from the bones of Psydon.
		<br><br>
		Fallen to insanity from the world industrialization and natural decimation. His followers struggle to find meaning in his words, to commune with the growing hostility seen within the previously-pristine glades and woods. He holds a deep resentment towards Malum, and his part in the aforementioned devastation.
		<br><br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> Ravox </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<span style="display:block; color:#774b44; font-size:120%"> "It's a rough world out there, kid. Keep safe. Stay holy." </span>
		<br>
		God of warfare, and justice. His domain encompasses righteous combat and bravery. He retreated to the underworld, reshaping it alongside Necra to include trials of combat and strife. He was crafted from the still-bloodied greatsword of Psydon.
		<br><br>
		Sullen and withdrawn, Ravox struggles to keep his followers away from Graggarism. As he has discovered, even remaining alive is not enough to avoid his words and domain twisted into heresy. Ravoxians often argue that their ideology lies closest to Psydon's own ways, though few if any remember to a degree of certainty.
		<br><br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> Malum</strong></summary>
		<br>
		<b><span style="display:block; color:#d39459; font-size:120%"> "Hammer with PURPOSE. Strike the STONE!" </span></b>
		<br>
		God of craft, forge, and toil. His domain encompasses architecture, mining, and sculpting. He considers the dwarven species his chosen, proudly supporting and guiding their technological improvements. He was crafted from the hands of Psydon.
		<br><br>
		Almost equally defined by conflict as much as creation, Malum and Dendor's rivalry spans millennia. Their bickering is reflected in the mortal planes through elven and dwarven skirmishes.
		<br><br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> Eora </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<span style="display:block; color:#e4bae6; font-size:115%"> "I will always love you, sweetheart. Regardless of the ne'er-do-wells you associate with."</span>
		<br>
		Goddess of love, family, and art. Her domain encompasses marriage, painting, singing, and literature- especially romance. She is considered the backbone of The Ten, barely able to keep them together and officially allied. She was crafted from the heart of Psydon.
		<br><br>
		Despite Astrata's overt support for same-sex couples, Eora remains begrudging at best. Some theorize it is hatred, some theorize jealousy. Regardless, her failed seduction of Pestra early in their history and the Great Insult exchanged has left a lasting effect on the so-called goddess of love.
		<br><br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> Xylix </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<i><span style="display:block; color:#dfe6ba; font-size:120%"> "Please, darling. The world aaaalways needs more DRAMA!"</span></i>
		<br>
		Diety of trickery, freedom, and inspiration. Their domain encompasses friendly pranks, barding, theatre, and performance. Known widely to be genderless, as they see such concepts only as masks to play a role. They were crafted from the tongue of Psydon.
		<br><br>
		Of all The Ten, Xylix is considered the most 'problem child.' Whether spats with Astrata over freedom, or making trouble for its own sake, they're something of an outcast from the orderly nature of the rest- and they are perfectly content with their position. For Xylixians, a boring life is not one worth living.
		<br><br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> Pestra </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<b><span style="display:block; color:#8aa01c; font-size:120%"> "What you see- loss? Imperfection? Illness? No. It is all just the changing cycles of the world. Strive to understand, fool." </span></b>
		<br>
		Goddess of disease, alchemy, and medicine. Her domain encompasses all cycles of change; from rot and decay to refinement and transformation. The domain of alchemy is a subject of dispute between Pestra and Noc frustratingly often for her taste, but she retains firm hold. She was a mistake, slithering out of Psydon's intestines left behind.
		<br><br>
		Despite closeness with Astrata, Pestra remains something of an outcast amongst The Ten due to her accidental origin. Her Great Insult of rebuffal towards Eora only further damaged ties, though Pestra thrives in her isolation. To her, it's simply less chains restraining experimentation.
		<br><br>
	</details>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> Necra </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<i><span style="display:block; color:#5a53d5; font-size:125%"> "Allow me serenity for once, child. A brief respite. Calamity surely strikes again soon..." </span></i>
		<br>
		Goddess of death and time. Once a close partner to Psydon. She was the first mortal, the first to die, and the second to achieve godhood. Her domain encompasses passing spirits, the underworld, and the cycle of reincarnation. She created the other Nine from her butchery of Psydon.
		<br><br>
		Necra is most controversial amongst The Ten. She is reviled for her butchery and perceived betrayal of Psydon by those faithful to Him, yet is defended by Tennites across the land claiming she acted out of necessity. The exact circumstances of Psydon's death are unclear, but Necra is surely responsible- and she makes no effort to hide it.
		<br><br>
	</details>

	<hr>
	<strong><span style="font-size:115%"> THE INHUMEN PANTHEON </span></strong>
	<br>
	<em> Mortals hold a right to divinity. We follow the new way. </em>
	<br><br>

	<details>
		<summary><strong> Matthios </strong></summary>
		<br>
		<span style="display:block; color:#17181a; font-size:120%"> "What I say is nonya business, asshole." </span>
		<br>
		God of thievery, ill-gotten gains, and highwaymen. A figure shrouded in mystery. While assumed to once have been humen, there are no records of his appearance. His very feats were spread by word-of-mouth and rumour, with little ties to any tangible figure. He was the first of The Four to ascend.
		<br><br>
		Matthios is used as a figurehead by bandits and oppressed peasants alike. While his exact beliefs are unknown, his hatred for nobility specifically is apparent. He would quench his insatiable greed with their coffers over most others. He is hailed as a figure of rebellion, of freedom similar to Xylix who resonates with those who've felt abandoned by The Ten.
		<br><br>
		Upon his ascension, an otherworldly tomb's entrances began appearing around the world. The Tomb of Matthios promised riches, rewards, but equal danger. Most who enter only manage to join his collection, or fall victim to the roaming bands of orcs and goblins within.
		<br><br>
	</details>

	<details>
